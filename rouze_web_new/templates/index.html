"The moment you ask someone what they want, they lie to you. Not maliciously. Not intentionally. But they lie nonetheless."

ROUZE exists because I got tired of watching companies make million-dollar decisions based on what people said they wanted, only to discover what people actually wanted six months too late.

Traditional market research operates on a simple premise: if you want to know what people think, ask them. But here's what I learned: people don't know what they think until they think it. And they don't think it when you're asking. They think it when they're complaining on Reddit at midnight, leaving 1-star reviews, or warning strangers in online forums.

These moments—unfiltered, unsolicited, unperformed—contain more truth than any survey ever could. The market is always signaling. Most companies just aren't listening.
