Listening Beats Asking

Traditional research asks what people think. ROUZE listens to what they're already saying when they don't know they're being observed.

The Problem With Asking

There's a peculiar phenomenon in market research: the moment you ask someone what they want, they lie to you. Not maliciously. Not intentionally. But they lie nonetheless.

Ask a customer if they'd pay $50 for your product, and they'll say yes. Watch what they actually do, and they'll balk at $30. Ask a patient if they're worried about a drug's side effects, and they'll give you the answer they think you want to hear. Check what they're Googling at 2 AM, and you'll see their real concerns.

Surveys don't capture truth. They capture performance-the version of themselves people present when they know they're being watched. This is the fundamental epistemological crisis of market intelligence: the act of asking changes the answer.

1

Revealed Preference

What people do tells you more than what they say. Watch behavior, not declarations. Actions under real conditions reveal true preferences.

Cross-Validation Methodology

We don't present isolated opinions as market truth. Every pattern in our analysis must be validated across multiple independent sources before inclusion.

Source Independence

Signals must appear in 2+ platforms with no cross-contamination. A Reddit thread and a G2 review count. Two Reddit posts don't.

Temporal Validation

Patterns must persist over time, not spike-and-disappear. We weight recency but require sustained signal presence.

Engagement Weighting

High-engagement signals (many upvotes, comments, shares) receive higher weight than isolated mentions.
