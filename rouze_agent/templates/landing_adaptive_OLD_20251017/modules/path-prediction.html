How we spot trends 3-6 months early

01

Track leading indicators, not lagging metrics

Most people look at sales data or news coverage (already too late). We look at signals that predict what's coming:

  • Developer activity: GitHub repos, Stack Overflow questions, npm downloads
  • Hiring patterns: Job postings requiring new skills or technologies
  • Early adopter chatter: Tech communities discussing emerging tools
  • Influencer interest: When thought leaders start experimenting
  • Feature requests: What users are asking for that doesn't exist yet
  • Regulatory signals: Policy discussions that will shape markets

Example: We spotted "sustainability packaging" sentiment surge 4 months before mainstream media coverage by tracking: → 340% increase in Reddit sustainability discussions
→ 23% of product reviews mentioning packaging waste
→ TikTok #sustainableliving views accelerating

02

Separate signal from noise

Not every trend is real. We validate with:

Volume acceleration Is conversation increasing week-over-week?
Cross-platform presence Does it appear across multiple communities?
Depth of engagement Are people having detailed discussions or just mentioning?
Commercial activity Are companies hiring for it? Investing in it?
03

Map the adoption curve

We estimate where the trend is in the lifecycle:

Experimentation Early adopters testing, no commercial traction 12-18 months out
Early adoption Startups building, enterprises watching 6-12 months out
Acceleration Mainstream companies investing, demand growing → Optimal entry window
Saturation Everyone has it, competitive advantage gone Too late
04

Give you the "when" not just the "what"

Knowing a trend is coming isn't enough. You need to know when to move:

Too early:
Market not ready, you burn cash educating customers
Optimal window:
Demand emerging, competition light, positioning advantage available
Too late:
Market saturated, you're playing catch-up, no differentiation
